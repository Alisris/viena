JESUS CHRIST SUPERSTAR - THE ROCK-MUSICAL IN CONCERT

THE BIG VBW EASTER CONCERT HIGHLIGHT 2023


From 31 March to 10 April 2023, Vereinigte Bühnen Wien present the world-famous cult rock opera by Andrew Lloyd Webber and Tim Rice as an impressive concert experience at the Raimund Theater!

 

JESUS CHRIST SUPERSTAR has been thrilling people all over the world for over four decades. The highly emotional and electrifying score by Andrew Lloyd Webber and Tim Rice has lost none of its relevance to this day.  The "Rock-Musical in Concert" returned to the Raimund Theater at Easter in the legendary VBW concert version sung in English. An absolute concert highlight with a large orchestra and musical star Drew Sarich in the role of "Jesus".

 

JESUS CHRIST SUPERSTAR tells the last seven days of Jesus' life in a unique way from Judas' point of view. It is about the Last Supper, Herod's judgement and Judas' betrayal, which leads to the Way of the Cross and finally to his death.

  • Drew Sarich

    Jesus

    Drew Sarich was born in St. Louis, Missouri and came to Europe in 1999 for the role of Quasimodo in the world premiere of Disney's THE HUNCHBACK OF NOTRE DAME. Since his debut at the Raimund Theater as Berger in HAIR, he has also been seen at VBW as Captain Sun in BARBARELLA, Crown Prince Rudolf in RUDOLF - AFFAIRE MAYERLING, Curtis in SISTER ACT, The Phantom in LOVE NEVER DIES and Che in EVITA. He embodied both Jesus and Judas in numerous productions of JESUS CHRIST SUPERSTAR, the title roles in JEKYLL & HYDE in Cologne and DRACULA in St. Gallen and was seen in HEDWIG AND THE ANGRY INCH in Berlin and as Cousin Kevin in THE WHO'S TOMMY at the Musical Sommer Amstetten. On Broadway he created the role of Armand in Elton John's LESTAT and Off-Broadway he starred in JAQUES BREL IS ALIVE AND WELL AND LIVING IN PARIS. As Jean Valjean, Drew Sarich was seen on both the West End and Broadway in LES MISÉRABLES. In Vienna and Berlin Drew Sarich played Count von Krolock in DANCE OF THE VAMPIRES and in Hamburg he created the title role in ROCKY - THE MUSICAL. At the Volksoper Wien he was seen as Antonio Vivaldi in the world premiere of VIVALDI - DIE FÜNFTE JAHRESZEIT, for which he was awarded the German Musical Prize in the category "Best Actor" and the Kronen Zeitung Readers' Prize in 2017. Drew Sarich was also seen as Molina in KUSS DER SPINNENFRAU at Bühne Baden. Most recently he played VIVALDI again and was seen in the role of Robert Baker in Leonard Bernstein's WONDERFUL TOWN at the Volksoper Wien. He was Daddy Brubeck in SWEET CHARITY and in the premiere series of INTO THE WOODS as Wolf and Cinderella's Prince at the Volksoper Wien. He recently embodied Guido Contini in the concert version of the musical production NEUN (NINE) at Bühne Baden. His three albums "Silent Symphony", "Let Him Go" and "Hunting For Heaven" are available on Spotify and Apple Music.

  • Raphael Gross

    Peter

    Raphael Groß was born in North Rhine-Westphalia in Germany. His musical education started in early childhood and he had his first piano lesson at the age of five. In the following years, he sang in the children’s choir of the Gospel Project-Ruhr, where he gained his first stage experience as a soloist. He began his vocal training at the age of 9, and since 2011 has been taking lessons under the tutelage of Prof. Michael David Mills (Folkwang University of the Arts, Essen). Even before attending stage school, Raphael took acting and singing lessons, and attended the summer school at the Laine Theatre Arts College in London. As a teenager, Raphael appeared in various stage productions and galas, but also gained a variety of TV experience, including an appearance on “Dein Song” (2010/2011; KiKa/ZDF). The show’s compilation album took him to fourth place in the German charts. Although passionate about performing, Raphael has also always worked on his own musical projects. At the age of 17, he composed the entire score for the musical DIE SCHREIBMASCHINE – DAS ERBE DES RUPERT BLOOMFIELD, in which he also played the lead role at venues including the KatiElli Theater in Datteln. He has been a student of Musicals at the Music and Arts Private University of the City of Vienna since 2015. He has also won a song contest held in honour of the Austrian composer Walter Jurmann, and in Vienna he has appeared as Rex in GRIMM! at the Renaissance Theater, directed by Werner Sobotka.

In addition to his operatic career, he can be heard as a soloist in concert, ensembles and choral formations. Most recently, he appeared at the Raimund Theater as Thuy in MISS SAIGON.

    Priester

    Florian Resetarits was born in Burgenland, Austria, and trained at the Music and Arts Private University of the City of Vienna, where he graduated with a Masters degree in 2017. He has been performing at festivals and in cabarets and concerts since he was a child. He has a permanent contract with the Festspiele Musical Güssing, where his roles have included the MC in CABARET, Jekyll and Hyde in JEKYLL UND HYDE, Ché in EVITA, Billy in CAROUSEL, Marius in LES MISÈRABLES and Fred Graham in KISS ME KATE. In the summer of 2013, he made his debut at the Lehár Festival Bad Ischl playing Rudolph in HALLO, DOLLY! and Pista in WHERE THE LARK SINGS, followed by Gaston in GIGI in 2014, Freddy in MY FAIR LADY in 2015, and Dr Blind in DIE FLEDERMAUS in 2016. A very versatile artist, he has also been a soloist at the Schubert Festival, held in the house where Schubert was born, has sung in concerts with the Pannonian Philharmonic, conducted by Prof. Alois Hochstrasser, and played the blacksmith in the chamber opera DER KLEINE SCHMIED VON RUHLA in the 2014 and 2015 seasons in Thüringen. From the autumn of 2016 until January 2017, he played Fridolin Weber/Thorwart in the VBW production of MOZART! in Duisburg and China.

  • Herbert Pichler

  • Herbert Pichler

    Herbert Pichler graduated from the University of Music and Performing Arts in Graz. He has been working as an artist in Vienna since 1989 and also teaches at the Institute for Popular Music at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna. He was musical director of, among others, MISS SAIGON, BARBARELLA, HAIR, MAMMA MIA! and BODYGUARD (all VBW), the new version of CABARET with Maria Bill (Volkstheater Wien) and the TV show "Ein Saitensprung" with Rainhard Fendrich. He has worked as a conductor for productions such as DER BAUER ALS MILLIONÄR (Landestheater Linz), "Musical!- Die Show" (ORF 2007) and I AM FROM AUSTRIA (VBW). Since the first season of the ORF show "Dancing Stars" he has also been the conductor of the Star Orchestra. Other examples of his varied musical activities include orchestral projects with Thomas Quasthoff, the mdw project CUBE, musical direction of the Nestroy Theater Prize, collaboration with the Mozarteum Chamber Orchestra and several projects with Herbert Pixner and the Tonkünstler Orchestra. As an arranger and musician, Herbert Pichler has worked on CD productions and TV shows with artists* such as Nina Hagen, Marianne Faithful and Chaka Khan, with whom he worked on the opening of the Vienna Festival in 2005, as well as with The Supremes, Harry Belafonte, Patricia Kaas, Placido Domingo, the Vienna Symphony Orchestra, the Vienna Radio Symphony Orchestra, Jos. Carreras, Luciano Pavarotti, Dave Stewart, Jim Steinman, Simply Red, Gloria Gaynor and many musical stars.

  • Andrew Voller

  • Andrew Voller

    Andrew Voller began his career programming Vari-Lite’s moving lights and in this capacity worked on productions of DANCE OF THE VAMPIRES (Vienna, Stuttgart, Hamburg), THE HUNCHBACK OF NOTRE DAME (Berlin), SING TO THE DAWN (Singapore) and BOMBAY DREAMS (London), as well as the opening ceremony for the Millennium Dome in London and the Eurovision Song Contest in 2001, 2009 and 2010. His design work includes INTO THE WOODS (Guildford School of Acting), SHREK - THE MUSICAL (tour of Germany), MOEDER, IK WIL BIJ DE REVUE (Utrecht), RENT (Guildford School of Acting), LIFT (Soho Theatre, London), SATURDAY NIGHT FEVER (tour of Holland, Milan), KEIN PARDON – DAS MUSICAL (Düsseldorf), HINTERM HORIZONT (Berlin), ALL SHOOK UP – LOVE ME TENDER (tour of Holland) and GREASE (St. Gallen). For the VBW, he was lighting designer for the gala WE ARE MUSICAL, as well as  LOVE NEVER DIES, THE PHANTOM OF THE OPERA and the new versions of ELISABETH, REBECCA, ROMEO & JULIET and HAIR. In Amstetten, he has created the lighting design for numerous productions, including THE WHO’S TOMMY, THE WILD PARTY, FOOTLOOSE, JESUS CHRIST SUPERSTAR, CARMEN CUBANA, ROCKVILLE, HAIR and AIDA. He was also lighting designer for Hans Klok’s magic shows FASTER THAN MAGIC (European tour) and THE BEAUTY OF MAGIC (Las Vegas). Andrew Voller also designed the lighting for ICH WILL SPASS! and BUDDY – THE BUDDY HOLLY STORY in Essen, as well as for the German tours of SATURDAY NIGHT FEVER, MIAMI NIGHTS and ICH WAR NOCH NIEMALS IN NEW YORK (previously seen in Hamburg, Vienna, Stuttgart, Zurich and Oberhausen) and most recently for EVITA at the Ronacher.

  • Patrick Polly

  • Patrick Polly

    Patrick Polly gained his first experience at ORF and then worked as a freelance sound engineer. He has been working at the Ronacher since 2008 and took over as head of sound engineering there in 2011. As FOH operator he supervised various productions at Vereinigte Bühnen Wien, including THE PRODUCERS, TANZ DER VAMPIRE, NATÜRLICH BLOND and MARY POPPINS. In the same capacity he toured internationally with WIEN MUSICAL CONCERT II and in Japan with ELISABETH IN CONCERT. He has also served as Assistant Sound Designer on SISTER ACT and ICH WAR NOCH NIEMALS IN NEW YORK, and as Associate Sound Designer on LOVE NEVER DIES and DER BESUCH DER ALTEN DAME. Since 2002 he has been supporting the association "Tonvoll" in all sound-related matters on a voluntary basis.
